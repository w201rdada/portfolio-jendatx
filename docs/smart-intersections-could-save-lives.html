2.1 Smart, Self-Sensing Intersections Could Save Lives

Every day, thousands of lives are lost worldwide when cars collide with each other and with other roadway users (pedestrians, cyclists, etc.)[Association for Safe International Road Travel]. Perhaps 50 Million people are injured in this way each year, causing untold pain and productivity loss. These causes point directly to a lack of information on-hand in the moment the information is needed - something that we in the so-called information age should be able to address.

Planners have long studied dangerous intersections where - time and time again - people and property get damaged, reports get written, debris is cleared away, and then traffic flow continuesâ€¦until the next crash. 2.2 The Data Science of Smart Intersections

The key to this projectâ€™s approach lies in the classification and characterization of roadway users according to mass (which, unlike orientation, should not change during a given roadway traversal) and velocity (which changes within predictable user-category bounds). The combination of the two is momentum - literally defined as mass in motion. Momentum is an especially appropriate factor for classification and prediction in a traffic risk-avoidance scenario because momentum is conserved in a collision: the wider the gap between two objectsâ€™ momentums, the more potential harm to the lower-momentum party.

For this pilot project, momentum would be determined for each roadway user approaching an intersection via semi-permanent installation of a series of inexpensive, weather-proof vibration-sensor packs. Each individual sensor-pack would be a combination of piezoelectric elements - converting various levels of vibration into various levels of voltage - and an array of vibration sensor switches - which either do or do not close a circuit, depending on the level of vibration impinging upon them. Initial placement is envisioned along the vertical walls of the existing curbing on all intersection approach paths. (After the pilot project demonstrates promise in accident avoidance with this method, further miniaturization and component hardening may allow the necessary components to be installed into standard roadway reflectors - bridging coverage to intersections without curbing.)

An intersectionâ€™s set of sensor-packs should be connected wirelessly to communicate their identities, placements, and vibration readings in real-time to the smart intersectionâ€™s own network hub, which itself would be connected to the internet for monitoring, maintenance, and in case of emergency reporting. Note that no personally-identifiable information would be transmitted (unlike with cameras); this fact alone should make public acceptance easier.

Once a pilot intersection is set up for detection and signal reception/connection, the Data Science team would supervise training of the smart intersection software so that the intersection can behave as a true node in the transit grid.
